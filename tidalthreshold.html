Tidal Threshold

Urla, İzmir, Turkey

2024 - 2025 Spring

Set against the evolving coastline of Urla İskele, the project reimagines heritage not as static preservation but as a vibrant, living part of daily life. As shifting sea levels and urban growth fragment access to historical layers, Tidal Threshold introduces an open, low-impact design approach that activates forgotten narratives and fosters public interaction. Connecting Urla İskele with Quarantine Island, this intervention serves as a threshold between past and future, land and sea, memory and movement. Here, architecture acts not only as shelter but as storyteller.

Studio Advisor
Zeynep Ege Odabaşı

Tools Used
Revit, Autocad, Photoshop, InDesign

Adaptive Spaces: Rediscovering the Sunken Past

As the sea gradually recedes over the coming centuries, the sunken city of Klazomenai—an ancient settlement dating back to 4000 BCE—will reemerge, unveiling its buried history. Tidal Threshold anticipates this shift by designing for a future coastline that extends into submerged zones. Today’s underwater ruins are envisioned as tomorrow’s courtyards, gardens, and gathering spaces. Architecture embraces time and nature as materials, creating a layered, dynamic landscape that evolves with the rhythm of the sea and erosion.

Layered Cultural Memory and Public Engagement

Urla İskele is a coastal district steeped in rich, multi-layered heritage shaped by ancient civilizations, Ottoman influences, and modern cultural exchanges. This cultural fabric, however, faces fragmentation due to changing water levels and urban expansion. Tidal Threshold delicately reveals archaeological traces through water-sensitive spatial sequences and interactive public zones, transforming heritage into a continuously engaging cultural experience. The project seamlessly weaves history into the present, fostering a deep connection between community, nature, and memory.

Flexible and Sustainable Public Spaces for the Future

The new masterplan reclaims Urla İskele’s fragmented, car-dominated shoreline by creating a continuous, walkable, and bike-friendly urban fabric. Designed with future sea-level fluctuations in mind, the project offers resilient, adaptable infrastructure that supports cultural continuity and encourages active public use. By harmonizing built and natural environments, Tidal Threshold fosters a sustainable, multi-layered urban ecosystem that shapes the present and future of coastal living.
