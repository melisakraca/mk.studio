About

Architecture, for me, is as much about asking the right questions as it is about finding the right forms. During my studies at Bilkent University, I’ve come to see design as a way of thinking—a tool to observe, interpret, and engage with the world around us. With a multicultural perspective, I approach architecture as both a creative pursuit and a problem-solving process. I’m drawn to projects that respond to context, embrace complexity, and challenge assumptions—balancing technical precision with a sensitivity to material, place, and people. As I step into the professional world, I see each design not just as a solution, but as an evolving dialogue between space, experience, and purpose. I aim to shape environments that are not only environmentally responsible, but also emotionally resonant. Locally rooted, globally minded, I remain open to collaboration, conversation, and continuous learning as I contribute to the future of the built environment.

Conservation of Historical Environments
Conservation of Urban Heritage: Triangle of Ministries, Ankara

This document is an academic study of the Triangle of Ministries in Ankara, tracing its original modernist layout (1924–1935), analyzing subsequent state-led interventions against international conservation charters, and proposing guidelines to restore the site’s integrity, authenticity, and public engagement.

History of Built Environment
Cinnah 19: Redefining Urban Space and Function

This paper is an academic study that investigates the architectural and historical significance of Cinnah 19, a key example of modernist residential architecture in Ankara. It analyzes the building’s design origins, evolution, spatial organization, and its role within the context of Turkey’s mid-20th-century housing developments. The paper aims to contribute to the understanding of cooperative housing models and modern architectural practices in Turkey, highlighting the cultural and functional importance of Cinnah 19.
