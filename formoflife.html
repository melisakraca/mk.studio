Form of Life

Ulus, Ankara, Turkey

2023 - 2024 Fall

Studio Advisor
Ph. D. Giorgio Gasco

Tools Used
Revit, Autocad, Rhino, Photoshop, InDesign, Lumion

The architectural project, influenced by the versatile ABC Design System from Spain, manifests as a harmonious fusion of form and function. Situated in a key area of Ulus near SuluHan, the building is thoughtfully designed as an accessible node in circulation. Flexible housing units inspired by this system converge to shape the building’s form, integrating residential spaces with strategically placed communal facilities. Emphasizing the balance between privacy and social interaction, the residences face a lush southern landscape, maximizing natural light and creating a serene atmosphere. Communal spaces located within the building masses foster a strong sense of community. Inviting gallery areas beneath and at junctions create dynamic entry points, welcoming residents and visitors alike. Rooftop terraces provide additional social spaces with panoramic views, encouraging interaction and connection.

Rooted in the ABC Design System from Spain—Armario (cabinet), Baño (bathroom), Cocina (kitchen)—the project organizes essential residential functions with adaptable modules. By minimizing permanent walls, spatial flexibility is maximized. Distinct living areas emerge through dynamic configurations of kitchen, bathroom, and cabinet modules, allowing circulation and openings to be reconfigured according to need. Privacy and space division are cleverly achieved through strategic placement of cabinetry zones rather than fixed partitions. Remaining areas remain open and malleable, giving residents freedom to personalize their environment. This approach creates a living space that balances functionality, adaptability, and user control, making the design responsive to changing lifestyles and needs.
