Artistry Sanctuary

Alsancak, İzmir, Turkey

2023 - 2024 Spring

Vision and Context

Artistry Sanctuary embodies a forward-thinking architectural vision that transforms the traditional long-term guest house into an inclusive and adaptive cultural hub. Situated within the vibrant urban fabric of the Alsancak Limanı three-corner district—a site collaboratively studied by the entire studio, where each part was individually analyzed and designed—the project reflects a collective and multifaceted approach. Initial steps were conducted in the spirit of a charette, fostering open collaboration. Subsequently, each designer developed their assigned area responding to diverse functions and needs, resulting in a cohesive yet layered architectural narrative. This dynamic setting fosters an environment where creativity flourishes alongside social engagement, making the sanctuary both a home and a platform for cultural exchange.

Studio Advisor
Ph. D. Giorgio Gasco

Tools Used
Revit, Autocad, Photoshop, InDesign, Lumion

Circulation and Spatial Experience

A carefully crafted circulation strategy promotes fluid movement throughout the sanctuary, enabling effortless transitions between private, semi-private, and public zones. This encourages spontaneous interactions between guests, artists, and local visitors, fostering a vibrant community atmosphere. Open floor concepts in public areas allow spaces to be dynamically adapted for exhibitions, performances, and social gatherings, ensuring that the sanctuary remains an ever-changing canvas for creativity.

Architectural Language and Social Connectivity

Artistry Sanctuary employs an architectural language that balances transparency with enclosure, creating an inviting yet intimate atmosphere. Elevated terraces and interstitial courtyards act as social nodes, encouraging participation and reflection. Far from being a mere shelter, the sanctuary functions as a living framework nurturing a creative ecosystem where diversity, connection, and shared experiences thrive—making it a vital cultural landmark within the urban landscape.

Flexible and Sustainable Public Spaces for the Future

The new masterplan reclaims Urla İskele’s fragmented, car-dominated shoreline by creating a continuous, walkable, and bike-friendly urban fabric. Designed with future sea-level fluctuations in mind, the project offers resilient, adaptable infrastructure that supports cultural continuity and encourages active public use. By harmonizing built and natural environments, Tidal Threshold fosters a sustainable, multi-layered urban ecosystem that shapes the present and future of coastal living.
